REAP Eligibility Criteria 2026: Complete Educational & Domicile Guide

The Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) 2026 is the gateway for students seeking admission into B.Tech and B.E. programs across various government and private engineering colleges in Rajasthan. Before applying for the counselling process conducted by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan, it is mandatory to ensure you meet all the prescribed conditions. Failing to meet the REAP eligibility criteria will lead to the cancellation of your candidature at any stage of the admission process.

The eligibility conditions are set to ensure that candidates possess the necessary academic background and fulfill the domicile requirements for state quota seats. In this comprehensive guide, we will break down the exact educational qualifications, age limits, medical fitness standards, and domicile rules you must satisfy to be eligible for REAP 2026.

Educational Qualifications for REAP 2026

The core of the REAP eligibility criteria lies in the candidate's academic performance in their Class 12th (or equivalent) board examinations. The guidelines are strictly aligned with AICTE norms. Here is what you need to qualify:

Category Minimum Required Percentage
General / UR Category 45% aggregate marks
SC / ST / Non-Creamy Layer OBC / MBC / PwD 40% aggregate marks

Mandatory Subjects: Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Along with these, they must have any one of the following optional subjects: Chemistry, Biotechnology, Biology, or Technical Vocational subjects.

Age Limit Criteria for REAP 2026

A major relief for engineering aspirants in Rajasthan is the age limit relaxation. As per the guidelines traditionally set by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), there is no specific upper age limit for candidates applying for the REAP counselling process. As long as you meet the educational qualifications and other criteria, you are eligible to participate, making it favorable for dropper students as well.

Domicile Requirements and Seat Matrix

REAP allocates seats based heavily on the domicile of the candidate. The reservation and priority rules are structured to favor bonafide residents of Rajasthan, though out-of-state students are also highly encouraged to apply.

  • Rajasthan State Candidates: Students who are bonafide residents of Rajasthan are eligible for the state quota, which usually accounts for about 85% of the seats in participating engineering colleges. They are also eligible for category-based reservations (SC/ST/OBC/MBC).
  • Outside Rajasthan Candidates: Candidates from other states are eligible to apply for the remaining 15% of the seats across private and some government-aided institutions. However, they are treated strictly under the General (UR) category, regardless of their reserved status in their home state.

Medical Fitness Requirements

Beyond academics, candidates must meet basic medical fitness standards to pursue an engineering degree. While no rigorous physical tests are conducted, candidates are required to submit a Medical Fitness Certificate signed by a registered Medical Officer at the time of reporting to their allotted institute. The certificate must state that the candidate is physically and mentally fit to undergo the rigors of an engineering program.

Basis of Admission (Merit List Criteria)

Being eligible does not guarantee a seat; admission is strictly merit-based. REAP usually prepares two separate merit lists:

  • JEE Main Merit List: Candidates who have a valid JEE Main 2026 score are given higher priority during the seat allotment process, especially for top government colleges.
  • Class 12th Merit List: For students who did not appear for JEE Main or have lower scores, a secondary merit list is prepared based on their Class 12th board percentage in the mandatory subjects.

It is highly recommended that candidates apply through their JEE Main score to maximize their chances of securing a premium seat.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on REAP Eligibility

Is there an age limit to apply for REAP 2026?

No, there is no upper age limit prescribed by CEG Rajasthan for candidates appearing in the REAP counselling process.

What is the minimum percentage required in Class 12 for REAP?

General category candidates need at least 45% aggregate marks in mandatory subjects (Physics, Math, and one optional subject), whereas reserved category candidates (SC/ST/OBC/MBC/PwD) need a minimum of 40%.

Can non-Rajasthan residents apply for REAP?

Yes, outside Rajasthan candidates are highly encouraged to apply. About 15% of the seats in private and some aided institutions are reserved for candidates from outside the state.

Is JEE Main mandatory for REAP 2026?

No, JEE Main is not mandatory. However, priority is given to candidates with valid JEE Main scores over those applying purely on the basis of Class 12th marks.
