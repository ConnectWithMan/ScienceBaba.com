About REAP 2026 Counselling

Updated: March 2026

The Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) is the centralized counselling process for admission to B.E./B.Tech/B.Arch courses in various government-aided and private unaided technical institutions of Rajasthan. This guide will walk you through every step of the REAP 2026 counselling.

What is REAP?

REAP is conducted by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan, on behalf of the Government of Rajasthan. It provides a single platform for students to apply for admission into top engineering colleges across the state based on their JEE Main scores or Class 12th marks.

Eligibility Criteria

To participate in REAP 2026, candidates must meet certain eligibility requirements. The key criteria are:

  • Educational Qualification: Passed 10+2 examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects along with one of the Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/Technical Vocational subjects.
  • Minimum Marks: Obtained at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category candidates) in the above subjects taken together.
  • Age Limit: There is no age limit for admission.
  • Domicile: Both Rajasthan domicile and out-of-state candidates are eligible, but priority is given to Rajasthan candidates.

Step-by-Step Counselling Process

The REAP counselling process is conducted online and involves several stages. Here’s a breakdown:

Step 1: Registration & Fee Payment

The first step is to register on the official REAP website. You will need to fill in your basic details, academic information, and pay the non-refundable counselling fee online.

Step 2: Choice Filling and Locking

After successful registration, you can log in to fill your choices of colleges and branches in order of preference. It is crucial to fill in as many choices as possible to maximize your chances of getting a seat. Remember to lock your choices before the deadline.

Step 3: Seat Allotment

Based on your rank (from JEE Main or Class 12th marks), category, and filled choices, the system will allot a seat. The allotment result will be published on the official website.

Step 4: Reporting to the Allotted Institute

If you are allotted a seat, you must download the allotment letter and report to the assigned institute within the specified dates for document verification and admission fee payment to confirm your seat.

Documents Required for Verification

You must carry the original and photocopies of the following documents to the reporting center:

  • REAP 2026 Allotment Letter
  • JEE Main 2026 Scorecard (if applicable)
  • Class 12th Marksheet and Certificate
  • Class 10th Marksheet and Certificate (for age proof)
  • Domicile Certificate
  • Category Certificate (if applicable)
  • Medical Fitness Certificate
  • Passport-sized photographs

Conclusion

The REAP counselling process is a straightforward but critical step towards securing your engineering seat in Rajasthan. By carefully following the steps, filling choices wisely, and keeping all documents ready, you can navigate the process smoothly. Good luck!

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is JEE Main score mandatory for REAP 2026?

No, a JEE Main score is not mandatory. Admissions through REAP are primarily based on Class 12th marks. However, candidates with a JEE Main score are given first priority in the seat allotment process.

Can I edit my choices after locking them?

No. Once you lock your choices for colleges and branches, you cannot make any changes. It is highly recommended to review your preferences carefully before locking them.

What happens if I don't get a seat in the first round?

If you are not allotted a seat in the first round, you will be automatically considered for subsequent rounds of counselling, such as the second round and the upward movement round, based on your existing choices.

What is the registration fee for REAP 2026?

The registration fee for REAP 2026 is typically Rs. 295/- (Rs. 250 + 18% GST), which must be paid online during the registration process. This fee is non-refundable.

Is there any reservation for out-of-state candidates?

A specific percentage of seats (usually around 15%) is reserved for out-of-state candidates in private engineering colleges, while government colleges primarily reserve seats for Rajasthan domicile candidates.

Do I need a domicile certificate if I completed my 12th in Rajasthan?

If you have studied in Rajasthan for the last 3 years consecutively up to the qualifying examination (12th), you might be exempted, but it is always highly recommended to have a domicile certificate ready as proof of state residency during document verification.
