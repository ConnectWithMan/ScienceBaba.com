REAP Choice Filling 2026: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

The Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) 2026 Choice Filling is undeniably the most critical phase of the entire engineering admission cycle in Rajasthan. While your JEE Main scores or Class 12th board marks dictate your position on the state merit list, it is your strategic choice filling that ultimately determines which college and branch you will be allotted. Conducted by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan, the choice filling window allows candidates to express their preferences for various B.Tech and B.E. programs across government, government-aided, and private engineering institutions in the state.

Many students secure a high merit rank but fail to get their desired institution simply because they make crucial errors during the choice filling and locking process. Whether you are aiming for top government colleges like MBM University Jodhpur, CTAE Udaipur, or leading private institutions in Jaipur, understanding the nuances of the REAP choice filling algorithm is essential. In this comprehensive, highly detailed guide, we will walk you through every single step of the REAP 2026 choice filling process, important dates, expert strategies to maximize your chances, and common mistakes you must absolutely avoid.

Important Dates for REAP 2026 Choice Filling

The CEG Rajasthan provides a strict, time-bound window for candidates to log in, select, arrange, and lock their choices. Missing this deadline means you will not be considered for the regular seat allotment rounds, regardless of how high your merit rank is. Below are the highly anticipated tentative dates for the REAP 2026 choice filling phase. Please note that these dates will be updated immediately once the official notification is released.

Commencement of REAP Option / Choice Filling First week of July 2026
Last Date to Add or Modify Choices Second week of July 2026
Last Date for Choice Locking Second week of July 2026
Auto-Locking of Saved Choices (If not done manually) Second week of July 2026 (Midnight)

Step-by-Step REAP Choice Filling Process 2026

The online choice filling process is designed to be user-friendly, but it requires careful attention to detail. Candidates must use the official REAP portal to complete this process. Here is the detailed, step-by-step breakdown of how to properly execute your choice filling:

Step 1: Login to the Candidate Dashboard

Visit the official REAP 2026 website. Locate the 'Candidate Login' section. You will need to enter your unique Form Number/Challan Number, Password, and Date of Birth. Once authenticated, you will be redirected to your personalized counselling dashboard where the 'Fill Choices' link will be activated.

Step 2: Advance College Fee Payment (If Applicable)

In previous REAP iterations, candidates were required to pay an advance college fee (usually around INR 10,000) before accessing the choice filling interface. This fee is later adjusted against your first-year tuition fee if you are allotted a seat. If you are not allotted any seat by the end of all rounds, this amount is generally refunded to the bank account provided during registration. Ensure your payment is successful before proceeding.

Step 3: Browsing and Adding Choices

Upon entering the choice filling interface, you will see a comprehensive list of all participating engineering colleges in Rajasthan, along with the specific branches they offer. You can use filters such as 'Government Colleges', 'Private Colleges', or search by specific branches like 'Computer Science' or 'Mechanical Engineering'. To add a college-branch combination to your list, simply click the 'Add' or '+' button next to it. There is absolutely no restriction on the maximum number of choices you can fill.

Step 4: Arranging the Priority Order

This is the most crucial step. The REAP seat allotment algorithm evaluates your choices starting strictly from Priority Number 1 downwards. Therefore, you must arrange your selected institutes in decreasing order of your preference. Use the 'Up' and 'Down' arrows provided on the portal to reorder your list. Your absolute dream college and branch should be at number one, followed by your second preference, and so on.

Step 5: Saving and Locking Choices

As you build and arrange your list, make sure to click the 'Save' button frequently to prevent data loss due to session timeouts or internet connectivity issues. Once you are 100% satisfied with your preference order, you must 'Lock' your choices. Once locked, no further additions, deletions, or modifications can be made. If you forget to lock them manually, the system will automatically lock your last saved choices at the deadline.

Expert Strategies for Smart Choice Filling

Securing the best possible seat requires more than just randomly selecting colleges. Employing a well-thought-out strategy can give you a significant advantage over other candidates. Here are some expert tips to optimize your REAP choice filling:

  • Analyze Previous Year Cutoffs Deeply: Before you even log into the portal, spend time analyzing the REAP opening and closing ranks from the last 2-3 years. This will give you a realistic idea of which colleges and branches fall within your merit range. Create a rough draft of your choices on paper first.
  • Fill Maximum Number of Choices: One of the biggest mistakes candidates make is filling only 5 to 10 choices. Because there is no limit, you should fill as many logical choices as possible. Even if you are confident about your rank, adding backup options ensures you are not left without a seat if cutoffs unexpectedly skyrocket.
  • The "Dream, Reach, and Safe" Approach: Structure your preference list into three tiers. The top 20% of your list should be your "Dream" colleges (those slightly above your expected cutoff). The middle 60% should be your "Reach" colleges (those where your rank historically matches the cutoff). The bottom 20% must be your "Safe" colleges (those where you are almost guaranteed admission).
  • Prioritize Branch vs. College Carefully: Decide early on whether you are willing to compromise on the college for a specific branch (like CSE), or if you are willing to take a lower-demand branch (like Civil or Production) just to get into a top-tier government college like MBM Jodhpur. Your priority order should reflect this decision consistently.
  • Be Careful with TFWS Seats: If you are eligible for the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) and have selected it during registration, you will see separate TFWS options for each branch. Always place the TFWS choice of a college strictly above the normal fee choice of the same college.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Thousands of students jeopardize their engineering careers due to easily avoidable errors during this phase. Ensure you steer clear of the following blunders:

  • Locking Choices Too Early: Do not lock your choices on the very first day. Save them and review them multiple times over a few days. Discuss your list with mentors or seniors before hitting the final lock button.
  • Listing Colleges Based on Alphabetical Order: Some students lazily select colleges as they appear on the screen. The REAP algorithm does not care; it will allot the first available seat from your top down. Never place a mediocre college above a top-tier one.
  • Ignoring College Location and Fees: Do not fill choices for private colleges located far away or those with exorbitant fees if you have no actual intention of joining them. If allotted, your refusal to join will complicate your participation in subsequent rounds.

What Happens After Choice Locking?

Once the choice filling deadline passes, the CEG Rajasthan will run its highly automated seat allotment algorithm. The system first checks your Priority 1. If your merit rank is high enough and a seat is available in your category, it is allotted to you. If not, the system moves to your Priority 2, then Priority 3, and so on, until it finds an available match.

The result will be published as the Round 1 Seat Allotment. You will then have the option to accept the seat, pay the reporting fees, and physically report to the college. If you are unsatisfied, you can apply for 'Upward Movement' to try for a higher preference in the next round, but only after reporting to your initially allotted institute.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on REAP Choice Filling

Can I change my choices after I have locked them?

No, once you click the 'Lock' button and confirm, your preference list is sealed. No further additions, deletions, or reordering can be done under any circumstances. Therefore, lock only when you are absolutely sure.

Is there a maximum limit to the number of choices I can fill in REAP 2026?

There is no maximum limit. You can fill as many college and branch combinations as are available to your eligibility. In fact, experts strongly recommend filling a high number of choices to secure a guaranteed seat.

What happens if I forget to lock my choices before the deadline?

If you have saved your preferences but failed to manually lock them before the specified deadline, the REAP portal will automatically lock your last saved choices. However, it is always safer to manually review and lock them.

Do I need to pay a fee specifically for choice filling?

Typically, candidates are required to pay an advance commitment fee (around INR 10,000) prior to unlocking the choice filling window. This is adjusted against college tuition if admitted, or refunded if no seat is allotted.
