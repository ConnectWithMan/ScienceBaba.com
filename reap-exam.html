REAP Exam 2026: Counseling & Admission Process

Every year, thousands of students search for details regarding the REAP exam to secure engineering admissions in Rajasthan. However, it is crucial to understand that the Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) is not a separate entrance examination. Conducted by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), REAP is a centralized counseling system that allocates seats based on pre-existing academic merit.

Is There a Separate REAP Entrance Exam?

No, there is no specific state-level REAP entrance exam conducted for admission to B.Tech, B.E., or B.Arch courses in Rajasthan. Instead, the seat allotment is done purely on a merit basis through the following channels:

  • JEE Main Scores: Candidates holding a valid JEE Main 2026 scorecard are given the absolute highest priority during the REAP seat allocation process.
  • Class 12th Board Marks: If engineering seats remain vacant after the JEE Main merit list is completely exhausted, they are filled based on the percentage scored in the 10+2 board examinations.

Important Dates for REAP 2026

Even though there is no specific REAP exam date, candidates must closely track the counseling schedule to ensure they do not miss out on admissions:

How to Apply for REAP 2026?

Since you don't need to study for a separate REAP exam, your main focus should be on the application and choice-filling process. Here are the steps to participate:

  • Step 1: Online Registration: Visit reap2026.in and register using your basic details to generate login credentials.
  • Step 2: Fee Payment: Pay the non-refundable counseling fee of INR 295/- via digital payment methods.
  • Step 3: Academic Details: Fill in your JEE Main score or Class 12th marks carefully, as this dictates your merit rank.
  • Step 4: Document Upload: Upload scanned copies of your mark sheets, domicile, and relevant reservation category certificates.
  • Step 5: Choice Filling & Locking: Select and prioritize your preferred engineering colleges and branches. Lock your choices before the deadline.

Eligibility Criteria for REAP 2026

To be eligible for the counseling process without a specific REAP entrance exam, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Must have passed the 10+2 (Class 12th) examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects.
  • Must possess at least 45% aggregate marks (40% for reserved categories of Rajasthan) in the qualifying examination.
  • A valid JEE Main 2026 score is highly recommended but not strictly mandatory.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: When is the REAP exam 2026 going to be held?
A: There is no specific REAP exam. Admissions are granted based on your JEE Main 2026 scores and Class 12th board examination marks.

Q: What is the syllabus for the REAP exam?
A: Since REAP is a counseling process and not a written test, there is no separate syllabus. To secure a high merit rank, candidates should follow and prepare for the JEE Main syllabus.

Q: Can I apply for REAP without taking the JEE Main exam?
A: Yes, candidates who have not taken the JEE Main exam can still apply for REAP counseling. Their admission will be processed based on their Class 12th board exam marks after all JEE Main applicants are allocated seats.
