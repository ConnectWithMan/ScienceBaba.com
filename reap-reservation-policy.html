REAP 2026 Reservation Policy: Category-wise Seat Matrix & Quota Rules

The Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) 2026 follows a strict reservation policy set by the Government of Rajasthan for admission to B.Tech and B.E. programs. The Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan, ensures that seats are distributed fairly among state-domiciled students, outside state students, and various reserved categories.

Understanding the REAP reservation policy is crucial for candidates during the choice-filling and counselling process, as it directly impacts seat allotment. Below is a detailed breakdown of the seat matrix, domicile quotas, and special reservations like TFWS and KM quotas.

State Domicile vs Out-of-State Quota

The primary division of seats in REAP is based on the geographical domicile of the candidates:

  • Rajasthan State Candidates (85% Quota): 85% of the total seats in government and private engineering colleges are reserved for candidates who are bonafide residents of Rajasthan. All category-based reservations apply only to these seats.
  • Outside Rajasthan Candidates (15% Quota): 15% of the seats are open for candidates from outside Rajasthan. These candidates are treated as General (UR) category.

Category-wise Reservation Matrix for Rajasthan Domicile

For the 85% seats reserved for Rajasthan domicile candidates, the state government guidelines mandate the following vertical reservation matrix:

Category Reservation Percentage
Scheduled Caste (SC) 16%
Scheduled Tribe (ST) 12%
Other Backward Classes (OBC) 21%
Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) 10%

Horizontal Reservations

Within the respective categories (vertical reservation), the following horizontal reservations are applied:

  • Women Candidates: 25% of seats are reserved horizontally for female candidates across all categories.
  • Persons with Disabilities (PwD): 5% of seats are reserved for physically handicapped candidates.
  • Ex-Servicemen / Defense Personnel (EXS): Priority is given to wards of defense personnel and ex-servicemen as per state guidelines.

Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) & Kashmiri Migrants (KM)

REAP also provisions extra seats above the regular intake for specific groups:

  • TFWS Quota: 5% supernumerary seats are available for candidates whose family's annual income is less than ₹8 Lakhs. These students are exempted from paying tuition fees.
  • KM Quota: 5% seats are reserved for wards of Kashmiri Migrants. They must possess a valid certificate from the competent authority.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on REAP Reservation

How many seats are reserved for outside Rajasthan students in REAP?

15% of the total seats in participating engineering colleges are reserved for candidates outside Rajasthan.

Can out-of-state students claim OBC/SC/ST reservation in REAP?

No, outside Rajasthan candidates cannot claim category reservation. They are considered under the General (Unreserved) category.

What is the TFWS quota in REAP counselling?

The Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) provides a 5% supernumerary seat quota for students with an annual family income of less than ₹8 Lakhs.

Is there a reservation for female candidates in REAP?

Yes, 25% of seats are reserved horizontally for female candidates across all categories (General, SC, ST, OBC, MBC, etc.).
