REAP Branch Wise Cutoff: Expected Ranks & Historical Trends

The Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) governs admissions to various B.Tech and B.E. programs across government, government-aided, and private engineering institutions in Rajasthan. After each allotment round, the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG) releases the official opening and closing ranks. A critical aspect of this process is the REAP Branch Wise Cutoff, as demand varies heavily depending on the engineering discipline chosen.

Understanding the branch-wise cutoff trends is crucial for candidates participating in the counselling process. It helps students structure their choice-filling strategy, deciding whether to compromise on the college for a highly competitive branch like Computer Science Engineering (CSE), or opt for core branches like Civil or Mechanical to secure a seat in top institutions like MBM Jodhpur or RTU Kota. In this comprehensive guide, we analyze the expected branch-wise cutoffs and factors affecting them.

How to Check REAP Branch Wise Cutoffs?

Once released, candidates can check the official cutoff list to see the closing ranks for specific branches across all colleges by following these simple steps:

Step 1: Visit the Official Website

Go to the official REAP portal governed by CEG Rajasthan.

Step 2: Navigate to Downloads/Notifications

Look for the 'Seat Allotment Cutoff' or 'Opening and Closing Ranks' link in the latest notifications or downloads section of the homepage.

Step 3: Select the Round

Click on the PDF link for the respective counselling round (e.g., Round 1, Round 2, or TFWS round). The board publishes separate PDFs for different rounds and categories.

Step 4: Search for Your Desired College and Branch

Open the PDF and use the search function (Ctrl+F or Cmd+F) to find your target college and branch (e.g., "Computer Science") to check the category-wise closing ranks. Compare it with your state merit rank.

Factors Affecting REAP Branch Cutoffs

The cutoff ranks for specific branches fluctuate every year based on several dynamic factors. Understanding these can help you better predict your chances:

  • Number of Applicants: An increase in the total number of students applying for REAP generally leads to higher cutoffs and stiffer competition.
  • Difficulty Level of JEE Main: Since REAP prioritizes JEE Main scores, the overall difficulty and scoring trends of the JEE Main exam directly impact the REAP merit list.
  • Availability of Seats: The total number of sanctioned seats for a specific branch in top colleges. The introduction of new tech branches (like AI or IoT) can slightly ease competition in core CSE.
  • Reservation Categories: Cutoffs vary significantly across different categories (General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS, etc.) and domicile statuses (Rajasthan state candidates vs. Out of State).
  • Branch Popularity: The popularity of certain branches (like Computer Science Engineering and IT) keeps their cutoffs consistently high year over year compared to core branches.

Types of Merit Lists for REAP Cutoff

CEG Rajasthan prepares separate merit lists, which eventually dictate the cutoffs for different applicant groups:

  • JEE Main Candidates: First priority is given to candidates with valid JEE Main scores. The cutoff for top colleges usually closes entirely within this list.
  • Class 12th Marks: Seats left vacant after exhausting the JEE Main merit list are filled based on Class 12th board percentiles.
  • TFWS Cutoff: A separate, usually much higher cutoff is released for candidates applying under the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS).

Expected REAP Cutoff Ranks for Top Colleges (Branch-wise)

Based on historical data and projected competition, here is an estimated safe rank range for securing admission (General Category, Rajasthan Domicile) in popular branches across top-tier government and private colleges in Rajasthan:

  • Computer Science & Engineering (CSE): Highly competitive. Safe Rank under top 1500 for Top Government (e.g., MBM) and top 3500 for Top Private (e.g., SKIT, JECRC).
  • Artificial Intelligence & Data Science: Safe Rank under top 2500 for Top Government and top 4500 for Top Private institutions.
  • Information Technology (IT): Safe Rank under top 3000 for Top Government and top 5000 for Top Private institutions.
  • Electronics & Communication (ECE): Moderate competition. Safe Rank under top 4000 for Top Government and top 7000 for Top Private.
  • Electrical Engineering: Safe Rank under top 5500 for Top Government and top 10000 for Top Private.
  • Mechanical Engineering: Lower competition. Safe Rank under top 7000 for Top Government and top 12000 for Top Private.
  • Civil Engineering: Lowest competition among core branches. Safe Rank under top 8000 for Top Government and readily available in most private colleges at lower ranks.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on REAP Branch Wise Cutoff

For more general queries regarding the registration and allotment process, be sure to visit our detailed REAP FAQ page.

Which engineering branch has the highest cutoff in REAP?

Computer Science Engineering (CSE) and related specializations like Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Data Science consistently have the highest cutoffs across all participating institutions.

Is the cutoff for core branches lower than CSE?

Yes, core branches like Civil, Mechanical, and Electrical Engineering generally experience much lower demand, meaning their closing ranks are significantly lower compared to IT-related fields.

Does the cutoff drop in subsequent counselling rounds?

Generally, yes. The closing ranks for most branches usually drop in Round 2 (Upward Movement) and significantly in the Spot/Special rounds as students withdraw to join other national institutes like NITs or IITs.

Where can I check the official REAP branch-wise cutoffs?

The official cutoff PDFs containing branch-wise opening and closing ranks for all colleges are published on the CEG Rajasthan's REAP portal after every allotment round in the notifications section.
